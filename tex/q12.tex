  \begin{abstract}
    The companion Gravity paper showed that the horizontal
    variation of the projective spectral entropy functional
    $S_\Pi[g] = \tfrac{1}{2}\log\det' A_g$ with respect to the base
    metric produces the Einstein tensor as the infrared-dominant response,
    through the Seeley--DeWitt coefficient~$a_2$.
    The present paper carries out the complementary vertical variation.
    Extending $A_g$ to a Laplace-type operator
    $A_{g,\mathcal{A}} = -(\nabla^{\mathcal{A}})^2 + E$
    on the associated vector bundle of the admissible principal fibre,
    with the connection and the fibre representation~$\rho$ held fixed,
    we compute the local logarithmic part of the Seeley--DeWitt
    coefficient~$a_4$ and isolate its gauge component, the Yang--Mills
    density $\tfrac{1}{12}\,\mathrm{tr}_\rho(F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu})$.
    Varying $S_\Pi[g, \mathcal{A}]$ with respect to the admissible
    connection at fixed base metric then yields the source-free
    Yang--Mills equations $D_\mu F^{a\mu\nu} = 0$.
    This local kinetic Yang--Mills sector, and its variation, are the
    robust result of the paper.
    The coefficient of the induced logarithm depends on the fibre
    representation content through the Dynkin index~$I_\rho$ and is
    therefore not universal.
    The physical gauge coupling $g_{\mathrm{YM}}$, like Newton's
    constant, remains a renormalized matching datum, of which only the
    logarithmic running is computed here; a single logarithmic
    divergence does not by itself establish asymptotic freedom, which
    depends on the full projected field content and is not claimed.
    What is structural, within the proper-time scheme used here, is the difference in
    ultraviolet divergence degree between the two sectors --- quadratic at~$a_2$ for gravity,
    logarithmic at~$a_4$ for gauge --- and not any predicted numerical hierarchy of the physical
    couplings; the contrast is not scheme-independent, the zeta-regularized determinant carrying
    no power divergences.
    The gauge group $G_\Pi = \mathrm{SU}(3)\times\mathrm{SU}(2)\times
    \mathrm{U}(1)$ is taken as input from the spectral admissibility
    sub-programme, whose $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ sector is established there.
    \emph{Interpretation.} The result suggests that, within this
    framework, the natural organisational space for unification may be
    spectral and variational rather than purely group-theoretic:
    gravity and gauge dynamics are separated by geometric direction
    (horizontal versus vertical) and by heat-kernel order.
    This reading is offered as an interpretive outlook, not as a
    theorem.
  \end{abstract}

  \section{Introduction}
    \label{sec:introduction}

    The Cosmochrony programme derives physical structure from a static
    relational substrate $\chi$ via a generally non-injective
    projection~$\Pi$~\cite{Beau2026m,Cosmochrony}.
    Two lines of development have reached sufficient maturity to be
    combined.
    The first, pursued in the Gravity paper~\cite{Beau2026h}, shows that the horizontal
    variation
    of the projective spectral entropy functional
    \begin{equation}\label{eq:spectral-entropy}
      S_\Pi[g]
      \;=\;
      \tfrac{1}{2}\log\det'\! A_g
    \end{equation}
    with respect to the base metric $g_{\mu\nu}$ reproduces the
    Einstein--Hilbert term as the infrared-dominant
    contribution~\cite{Beau2026h}.
    The second, pursued in the Q-series from Q6a onward, derives the
    admissible gauge group $G_\Pi$, gauge charges, and the Yang--Mills
    principle from the fibre structure of~$\Pi$~\cite{Beau2026q6a}.

    These two results share a common object, $A_g$, but exploit it
    in orthogonal directions.
    The present paper unifies them by extending $A_g$ to a
    Laplace-type operator $A_{g,\mathcal{A}}$ on the total associated
    bundle of $\Pi$ and computing both the gravitational ($a_2$) and
    the gauge ($a_4$) sectors of the spectral entropy from a single
    functional.

    \subsection{Main result}
      \label{subsec:main-result}

      The central result is the following.

      \begin{theorem}[Yang--Mills from $a_4$]
        \label{thm:main}
        Let $G_\Pi$ be the admissible gauge group derived
        in~\cite{Beau2026q6a,Beau2026a35}, and let
        $A_{g,\mathcal{A}}$ be the Laplace-type operator on the
        associated vector bundle constructed in
        Section~\ref{sec:operator}.
        Then:
        \begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
        \item The local part of the Seeley--DeWitt coefficient
        $a_4(A_{g,\mathcal{A}})$ contains the Yang--Mills density,
        carried by the gauge component
        $\Omega^{\mathrm{gauge}}_{\mu\nu} = F_{\mu\nu}$ of the total
        bundle curvature:
        \[
          a_4(A_{g,\mathcal{A}})
          \;\supset\;
          \int
          \frac{1}{12}\,
          \mathrm{tr}_\rho(F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu})
          \sqrt{g}\,\mathrm{d}^4 x\,,
        \]
        the overall factor $(4\pi)^{-2}$ being carried once by the
        heat-kernel prefactor of
        Equation~\eqref{eq:heat-kernel-expansion}.
        \item The corresponding local logarithmic part of the
        projective spectral entropy is
        \[
          S_\Pi[g, \mathcal{A}]
          \;\supset\;
          -\,\frac{1}{12 \cdot 16\pi^2}\,
          \log\!\left(\frac{\Lambda}{\mu}\right)
          \int
          \mathrm{tr}_\rho(F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu})
          \sqrt{g}\,\mathrm{d}^4 x\,,
        \]
        the sign being fixed by the convention
        $S_\Pi = +\tfrac{1}{2}\log\det' A_{g,\mathcal{A}}$.
        \item At fixed base metric, the vertical variation of $S_\Pi$
        yields the Yang--Mills equations in the current-free sector:
        \[
          \frac{\delta S_\Pi}{\delta \mathcal{A}^a_\nu}
          \;=\; 0
          \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
          D_\mu F^{a\mu\nu} = 0\,.
        \]
        \end{enumerate}
      \end{theorem}

      These three statements follow from standard heat-kernel
      technology~\cite{Vassilevich2003,ParkerToms2009} applied to the
      operator $A_{g,\mathcal{A}}$, once the base--fibre structure and
      the admissibility of $\mathcal{A}$ are established.

  \section{Base--fibre decomposition of the projective operator}
    \label{sec:decomposition}

    \subsection{The admissible principal bundle}
      \label{subsec:bundle}

      The non-injective projection $\Pi : \chi \to O$ of
      Foundation~M~\cite{Beau2026m}, from the static relational
      substrate~$\chi$ to the observable space~$O$, carries a natural
      base--fibre structure.
      Throughout, $\chi$ denotes the substrate and the symbol
      $\Omega_{\mu\nu}$ is reserved for the bundle curvature two-form
      introduced in Section~\ref{subsec:a4-full}.
      The effective base space $M$ is the Riemannian manifold derived
      in Q5b~\cite{Beau2026q6b} and Q11~\cite{Beau2026q11}:
      the BFS shell stratification of $\mathrm{Heis}_3(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$
      converges in the Gromov--Hausdorff sense to a Carnot--Carathéodory
      structure with homogeneous dimension $D_{\mathrm{hom}} = 4$
      whose effective metric closure is Lorentzian,
      as established in~\cite{Beau2026q11}.
      The effective fibre is the admissible gauge group
      $G_\Pi = \mathrm{SU}(3) \times \mathrm{SU}(2) \times \mathrm{U}(1)$
      derived in~\cite{Beau2026q6a,Beau2026a35} as the composite of
      three admissibility fixed points of orbit sizes~1, 2,~and~3.

      \begin{definition}[Admissible principal bundle]
        \label{def:bundle}
        The admissible principal bundle associated with the projection
        $\Pi$ is the principal $G_\Pi$-bundle
        $\pi : P \to M$, with structure group $G_\Pi$ and
        connection~$\mathcal{A}$, constructed as follows.
        The total space $P$ is the fibre product of the three orbit
        sub-bundles identified in~\cite{Beau2026q6a,Beau2026a35}.
        The connection $\mathcal{A} \in \Omega^1(P, \mathfrak{g}_\Pi)$
        is the unique $\mathrm{ad}$-equivariant horizontal distribution
        compatible with the admissibility constraints of
        Foundation~M~\cite{Beau2026m}.
        The curvature $F_{\mu\nu}
        = \partial_\mu \mathcal{A}_\nu
        - \partial_\nu \mathcal{A}_\mu
        + [\mathcal{A}_\mu, \mathcal{A}_\nu]$
        takes values in $\mathfrak{g}_\Pi$.
      \end{definition}

    \subsection{The associated vector bundle}
      \label{subsec:associated}

      Let $\rho : G_\Pi \to \mathrm{GL}(V)$ be a representation of
      $G_\Pi$ on a finite-dimensional complex vector space~$V$.
      The associated vector bundle is
      $\mathcal{V} = P \times_\rho V \to M$.
      Sections of~$\mathcal{V}$ carry the combined action of the base
      geometry (through the Levi-Civita connection~$\nabla^g$) and the
      fibre gauge field (through $\mathcal{A}$).
      The covariant derivative on $\Gamma(\mathcal{V})$ is
      \begin{equation}\label{eq:covariant-derivative}
        \nabla^\mathcal{A}_\mu
        \;=\;
        \partial_\mu
        + \omega_\mu
        + \mathcal{A}_\mu\,,
      \end{equation}
      where $\omega_\mu$ is the spin connection on~$M$.

    \subsection{Vertical variation at fixed base metric}
      \label{subsec:decoupling}

      The Yang--Mills equation of Section~\ref{sec:variation} is
      obtained by varying the connection at fixed base metric.
      The following lemma records what this fixed-metric restriction
      does, and does not, decouple.

      \begin{lemma}[Vertical variation at fixed base metric]
        \label{lem:decoupling}
        Under minimal coupling, i.e.\ when the endomorphism $E$ does
        not depend on the connection $\mathcal{A}$, the coefficient
        $a_2(A_{g,\mathcal{A}})$ is independent of $\mathcal{A}$, and
        the connection-dependent part of $a_4$ enters only through the
        gauge invariant $\mathrm{tr}_\rho(F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu})$.
        Consequently the vertical variation
        $\delta_\mathcal{A} S_\Pi$ at fixed $g$ isolates the
        Yang--Mills sector and receives no contribution from the
        $a_2$ Einstein sector.
      \end{lemma}

      \begin{proof}
        The Seeley--DeWitt coefficients $a_{2k}(A_{g,\mathcal{A}})$
        are local integrals of invariants of degree~$2k$ in
        derivatives of $g_{\mu\nu}$ and
        $\mathcal{A}_\mu$~\cite{Vassilevich2003}.
        At minimal coupling, $a_2$ depends only on $g$ and on the
        $\mathcal{A}$-independent endomorphism $E$
        (Equation~\eqref{eq:a2}), so $\delta_\mathcal{A} a_2 = 0$.
        The leading connection-dependent contribution to $a_4$ is the
        gauge invariant $\mathrm{tr}_\rho(\Omega^{\mathrm{gauge}}_{\mu\nu}
        \Omega^{\mathrm{gauge}\,\mu\nu})$; its variation with respect
        to $\mathcal{A}$, at fixed $g$, is the Yang--Mills divergence
        $D_\mu F^{\mu\nu}$ of Section~\ref{sec:variation}, with no
        admixture from the gravitational invariants.
      \end{proof}

      \begin{remark}[No full horizontal--vertical decoupling]
        \label{rem:no-full-decoupling}
        The restriction to fixed $g$ is essential: the two sectors are
        \emph{not} independent under a joint variation.
        The gauge term $\mathrm{tr}_\rho(F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu})
        = g^{\mu\rho}g^{\nu\sigma}\,
        \mathrm{tr}_\rho(F_{\mu\nu}F_{\rho\sigma})$ depends on the
        metric through the index contractions and the volume element,
        so its \emph{horizontal} variation $\delta_g$ is precisely the
        Yang--Mills energy--momentum tensor
        $T^{\mathrm{YM}}_{\mu\nu}$ --- an $a_4$-order contribution that
        sources the Einstein sector, not an $a_6$ effect.
        This gauge--gravity back-reaction is therefore a genuine
        coupling already at $a_4$; its treatment belongs to the joint
        Einstein--Yang--Mills problem treated in Q13, and it is
        neither needed for, nor does it affect, the fixed-metric
        derivation of $D_\mu F^{\mu\nu} = 0$ given here.
        Curvature couplings of the form
        $E \supset \kappa\,F_{\mu\nu}g^{\mu\rho}g^{\nu\sigma}$ inside
        $E$ itself are separately excluded by the minimal-coupling
        choice adopted in Section~\ref{sec:operator}.
      \end{remark}

  \section{Laplace-type operator with admissible fibre connection}
    \label{sec:operator}

    \subsection{Construction of the operator}
      \label{subsec:operator-construction}

      The spectral entropy functional of the Gravity
      paper~\cite{Beau2026h} is extended to the associated
      bundle $\mathcal{V}$ by replacing $A_g$ with the operator:
      \begin{equation}\label{eq:total-operator}
        A_{g,\mathcal{A}}
        \;=\;
        -(\nabla^\mathcal{A})^2 + E\,,
      \end{equation}
      acting on $L^2(M, \mathcal{V})$.
      Here $(\nabla^\mathcal{A})^2
  = g^{\mu\nu}\nabla^\mathcal{A}_\mu \nabla^\mathcal{A}_\nu$
      is the connection Laplacian, and $E \in \mathrm{End}(\mathcal{V})$
      is the endomorphism encoding the admissibility correction:
      \begin{equation}\label{eq:endomorphism}
        E
        \;=\;
        E_{\mathrm{adm}}\,,
      \end{equation}
      where $E_{\mathrm{adm}} \in \mathrm{End}(\mathcal{V})$ encodes
      the admissibility constraints of the projection fibre.
      We deliberately do \emph{not} add a conformal
      $\tfrac{R}{6}\,\mathrm{id}_\mathcal{V}$ term to $E$: with the
      $a_2$ coefficient of Equation~\eqref{eq:a2}, in which $E$ enters
      as $-E$, such a term would cancel exactly the scalar-curvature
      contribution $\tfrac{R}{6}\,\mathrm{id}$ and remove the very
      Einstein sector that the horizontal variation is meant to
      reproduce. Omitting it keeps
      $a_2 \supset \tfrac{R}{6}\,\mathrm{tr}(\mathrm{id})$ and makes
      $A_{g,\mathcal{A}}$ reduce, at $\mathcal{A}=0$, to the minimal
      operator $A_g$ of the Gravity paper~\cite{Beau2026h}.

    \subsection{Self-adjointness and non-negativity}
      \label{subsec:selfadjoint}

      \begin{proposition}
        \label{prop:self-adjoint}
        The operator $A_{g,\mathcal{A}}$ is self-adjoint and
        non-negative on $L^2(M, \mathcal{V})$ for any admissible
        connection $\mathcal{A}$ and any $E \geq 0$.
      \end{proposition}

      \begin{proof}
        The connection Laplacian $-(\nabla^\mathcal{A})^2$ is
        self-adjoint and non-negative by standard functional
        analysis~\cite{ParkerToms2009}.
        The endomorphism $E = E_{\mathrm{adm}}$ is self-adjoint by
        construction, being symmetric under the admissibility
        constraints of~\cite{Beau2026m}.
        Non-negativity for $E \geq 0$ follows from the Bochner identity.
      \end{proof}

    \subsection{The extended spectral entropy}
      \label{subsec:extended-entropy}

      The spectral entropy functional is extended to:
      \begin{equation}\label{eq:extended-entropy}
        S_\Pi[g, \mathcal{A}]
        \;=\;
        \tfrac{1}{2}\,\log\det'\! A_{g,\mathcal{A}}\,,
      \end{equation}
      where $\det'$ denotes the zeta-regularized determinant
      excluding the zero modes.
      When $\mathcal{A} = 0$ and $E = E_{\mathrm{adm}}$,
      this reduces to the functional of~\cite{Beau2026h}.

  \section{Heat-kernel coefficient \(a_4\) and the Yang--Mills term}
    \label{sec:a4}

    \subsection{Seeley--DeWitt expansion}
      \label{subsec:sdw-expansion}

      For the operator $A_{g,\mathcal{A}}$ of
      Equation~\eqref{eq:total-operator}, the heat-kernel
      expansion as $t \to 0^+$ takes the standard
      form~\cite{Vassilevich2003,ParkerToms2009}:
      \begin{equation}\label{eq:heat-kernel-expansion}
        \mathrm{Tr}\!\left(e^{-t\,A_{g,\mathcal{A}}}\right)
        \;\sim\;
        (4\pi t)^{-2}
        \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} t^k\, a_{2k}(A_{g,\mathcal{A}})\,,
        \quad
        t \to 0^+\,,
      \end{equation}
      where in four dimensions:
      \begin{align}
        a_0
        &= \int \mathrm{tr}(\mathrm{id})\,\sqrt{g}\,\mathrm{d}^4 x\,,
        \label{eq:a0} \\
        a_2
        &= \int \mathrm{tr}\!\left(
                               \frac{R}{6}\,\mathrm{id} - E
        \right)\sqrt{g}\,\mathrm{d}^4 x\,,
        \label{eq:a2}
      \end{align}
      and $a_4$ is given in
      Section~\ref{subsec:a4-full} below.

    \subsection{The \texorpdfstring{$a_2$}{a2} coefficient
    and the Einstein sector}
      \label{subsec:a2-einstein}

      With $E = E_{\mathrm{adm}}$ (no conformal term, see
      Section~\ref{subsec:operator-construction}), the $a_2$
      coefficient retains its scalar-curvature contribution,
      \begin{equation}\label{eq:a2-minimal}
        a_2(A_{g,\mathcal{A}})
        \;=\;
        \int \mathrm{tr}\!\left(
          \tfrac{R}{6}\,\mathrm{id}_\mathcal{V}
          - E_{\mathrm{adm}}
        \right)\sqrt{g}\,\mathrm{d}^4 x\,,
      \end{equation}
      and is independent of the gauge connection $\mathcal{A}$.
      Under the identification of the effective metric established
      in~\cite{Beau2026h}, the horizontal variation
      $\delta_{g} S_\Pi = \delta_g[\tfrac{1}{2}\log\det'
      A_{g,\mathcal{A}}]$ of the $R$-dependent part reproduces the
      Einstein tensor as the infrared-dominant contribution; this is
      the result of the Gravity paper~\cite{Beau2026h} and is not
      repeated here.
      By Lemma~\ref{lem:decoupling}, $a_2$ is not modified by the
      gauge connection; the converse gauge-to-gravity back-reaction
      (Remark~\ref{rem:no-full-decoupling}) enters only through the
      metric variation of the $a_4$ term and is treated in Q13.

    \subsection{The \texorpdfstring{$a_4$}{a4} coefficient}
      \label{subsec:a4-full}

      The full $a_4$ coefficient for the operator
      $D = -(\nabla^\mathcal{A})^2 + E$ acting on sections of the
      vector bundle, written with the total bundle curvature
      $\Omega_{\mu\nu} = [\nabla^\mathcal{A}_\mu,
      \nabla^\mathcal{A}_\nu]$ and in the normalisation of
      Equation~\eqref{eq:heat-kernel-expansion} (the overall
      $(4\pi)^{-2}$ residing once in the heat-kernel prefactor,
      not repeated here), is~\cite{Vassilevich2003}:
      \begin{align}\label{eq:a4-full}
        a_4(D)
        &=
        \int \mathrm{tr}\!\Bigl[
          \tfrac{1}{12}\,
          \Omega_{\mu\nu}\Omega^{\mu\nu}
          + \tfrac{1}{2} E^2
          - \tfrac{1}{6} R E
          \notag\\
        &\qquad\qquad
          + \tfrac{1}{180}\bigl(
          5 R^2
          - 2 R_{\mu\nu}R^{\mu\nu}
          + 2 R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}R^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}
          \bigr)\,\mathrm{id}
          + \text{total derivatives}
          \Bigr]
        \sqrt{g}\,\mathrm{d}^4 x\,,
      \end{align}
      where the coefficient $\tfrac{1}{12}$ of the curvature-squared
      invariant is the reduced form of Vassilevich's
      $30\,\Omega^2/360$~\cite{Vassilevich2003}.
      The terms involving $R$, $R_{\mu\nu}$,
      $R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}$ are purely gravitational and
      do not depend on $\mathcal{A}$.
      The term $\tfrac{1}{2}E^2$ contributes to the cosmological
      sector and to the admissibility renormalization.

    \subsection{Isolation of the gauge component of the curvature}
      \label{subsec:curvature-identification}

      The total curvature $\Omega_{\mu\nu}
      = [\nabla^\mathcal{A}_\mu, \nabla^\mathcal{A}_\nu]$ of the
      connection~\eqref{eq:covariant-derivative} is \emph{not} the
      gauge field strength alone: it also contains the Riemannian
      (spin-connection) curvature. On the associated bundle
      $\mathcal{V} = \mathcal{V}_{\mathrm{spin}} \otimes
      \mathcal{V}_\rho$ it splits as
      \begin{equation}\label{eq:curvature-split}
        \Omega_{\mu\nu}
        \;=\;
        \Omega^{\mathrm{spin}}_{\mu\nu}\otimes \mathrm{id}_\rho
        \;+\;
        \mathrm{id}_{\mathrm{spin}}\otimes
        \Omega^{\mathrm{gauge}}_{\mu\nu}\,,
        \qquad
        \Omega^{\mathrm{gauge}}_{\mu\nu} = F_{\mu\nu}
        = \partial_\mu \mathcal{A}_\nu
        - \partial_\nu \mathcal{A}_\mu
        + [\mathcal{A}_\mu, \mathcal{A}_\nu]\,,
      \end{equation}
      where $\Omega^{\mathrm{spin}}$ is built from the Riemann tensor
      and feeds only the purely gravitational invariants already
      displayed in~\eqref{eq:a4-full}.
      The cross term
      $\mathrm{tr}(\Omega^{\mathrm{spin}}\Omega^{\mathrm{gauge}})$
      vanishes because $\mathrm{tr}_\rho F_{\mu\nu} = 0$ for a
      semisimple gauge algebra, so the curvature-squared invariant
      separates cleanly. Retaining the gauge part only:
      \begin{equation}\label{eq:a4-gauge}
        a_4(A_{g,\mathcal{A}})\big|_{\mathrm{gauge}}
        \;=\;
        \int
        \frac{1}{12}\,
        \mathrm{tr}_\rho(F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu})
        \sqrt{g}\,\mathrm{d}^4 x\,,
      \end{equation}
      where $\mathrm{tr}_\rho$ is the trace in the fibre representation
      $\rho$ of $G_\Pi$. If the fibre also carries Lorentz (spin)
      indices, a positive integer multiplicity $\dim\mathcal{V}_{\mathrm{spin}}$
      multiplies this coefficient; it is absorbed into the
      representation-dependent normalisation below and does not affect
      the field equation.
      The representation dependence is carried by the Dynkin index
      $I_\rho$,
      \begin{equation}\label{eq:dynkin}
        \mathrm{tr}_\rho(T^a T^b) = I_\rho\,\delta^{ab}\,,
        \qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad
        \mathrm{tr}_\rho(F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu})
        = I_\rho\, F^a_{\mu\nu}F^{a\,\mu\nu}\,,
      \end{equation}
      so the normalisation of the induced kinetic term is fixed by the
      representation content through $I_\rho$ and is \emph{not}
      universal.
      Equation~\eqref{eq:a4-gauge}, together with the identification
      $\Omega^{\mathrm{gauge}}_{\mu\nu} = F_{\mu\nu}$, establishes
      part~(i) of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}.

  \section{Vertical variation and Yang--Mills equations}
    \label{sec:variation}

    \subsection{The spectral entropy and its renormalized form}
      \label{subsec:renormalized}

      The zeta-regularized spectral entropy~\eqref{eq:extended-entropy}
      is related to the heat kernel by:
      \begin{equation}\label{eq:zeta-entropy}
        S_\Pi[g, \mathcal{A}]
        \;=\;
        -\tfrac{1}{2}\,\zeta'_{A_{g,\mathcal{A}}}(0)\,,
      \end{equation}
      where $\zeta_D(s) = \mathrm{Tr}(D^{-s})$ is the spectral
      zeta function.
      After proper-time regularization with cutoff~$\Lambda$ and
      renormalization scale $\mu$, the $t^0$ (logarithmic) part of
      $S_\Pi = +\tfrac{1}{2}\log\det' A_{g,\mathcal{A}}
      = -\tfrac{1}{2}\zeta'_{A_{g,\mathcal{A}}}(0)$ picks up the
      gauge contribution
      \begin{equation}\label{eq:entropy-YM}
        S_\Pi[g, \mathcal{A}]
        \;\supset\;
        -\,\frac{I_\rho}{12 \cdot 16\pi^2}\,
        \log\!\left(\frac{\Lambda}{\mu}\right)
        \int
        F^a_{\mu\nu}F^{a\,\mu\nu}
        \sqrt{g}\,\mathrm{d}^4 x\,,
      \end{equation}
      the overall minus sign coming from
      $S_\Pi = -\tfrac{1}{2}\zeta'(0)$ acting on a positive $t^0$
      heat-kernel coefficient, i.e.\ fixed by the convention
      $S_\Pi = +\tfrac{1}{2}\log\det'$.
      This establishes part~(ii) of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}.
      The logarithmic form marks a marginal, four-dimensional
      renormalizable-type coupling; whether the induced running is
      asymptotically free depends on the sign of the full
      $\beta$-function --- hence on the complete projected field
      content --- and is not settled by this single contribution.

    \subsection{Vertical variation}
      \label{subsec:vertical-variation}

      The variation of $S_\Pi[g, \mathcal{A}]$ with respect to the
      gauge connection $\mathcal{A}^a_\mu$ is obtained from
      \eqref{eq:entropy-YM} via:
      \begin{align}\label{eq:vertical-variation}
        \delta_\mathcal{A}
        \left[F^a_{\mu\nu}F^{a\,\mu\nu}\right]
        &=
        2\,F^{a\,\mu\nu}\,\delta F^a_{\mu\nu}
        \notag\\
        &=
        2\,F^{a\,\mu\nu}
            (D_\mu \delta\mathcal{A}^a_\nu
            - D_\nu \delta\mathcal{A}^a_\mu)
        \notag\\
        &=
        4\,F^{a\,\mu\nu} D_\mu \delta\mathcal{A}^a_\nu\,.
      \end{align}
      Integrating by parts (using $\nabla_\mu\sqrt{g} = 0$):
      \begin{equation}\label{eq:integration-by-parts}
        \int F^{a\,\mu\nu} D_\mu \delta\mathcal{A}^a_\nu
        \,\sqrt{g}\,\mathrm{d}^4 x
        =
        -\int (D_\mu F^{a\mu\nu})\,\delta\mathcal{A}^a_\nu
        \,\sqrt{g}\,\mathrm{d}^4 x
        + \text{boundary terms}\,.
      \end{equation}
      Therefore, with the representation-dependent constant
      $c_{\mathrm{YM}}
      = I_\rho\,\log(\Lambda/\mu)\,/\,(3 \cdot 16\pi^2) > 0$,
      \begin{equation}\label{eq:variation-entropy}
        \frac{\delta S_\Pi}{\delta \mathcal{A}^a_\nu}
        \;=\;
        -\,c_{\mathrm{YM}}\,
        (D_\mu F^{a\mu\nu})\,\sqrt{g}\,.
      \end{equation}

    \subsection{Yang--Mills equations}
      \label{subsec:YM-equations}

      In the absence of projected matter currents
      (the ``current-free sector''), the variational
      principle $\delta_\mathcal{A} S_\Pi = 0$ yields:
      \begin{equation}\label{eq:YM-equations}
        \boxed{D_\mu F^{a\mu\nu} = 0\,,}
      \end{equation}
      which are the Yang--Mills equations for the gauge group $G_\Pi$
      in vacuo.
      This establishes part~(iii) of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}.

      \begin{remark}
        When projected matter fields $\psi$ are included,
        the variation of the matter coupling term in $S_\Pi$
        contributes a current $J^{a\nu} = \delta S_{\mathrm{matter}}
        / \delta \mathcal{A}^a_\nu$, and the full equations
        become $D_\mu F^{a\mu\nu} = J^{a\nu}$.
        The derivation of the matter current from the projective
        structure lies beyond the scope of the present paper.
      \end{remark}

      \begin{remark}
        The Yang--Mills equations~\eqref{eq:YM-equations} emerge
        from $S_\Pi$ via the $a_4$ coefficient, which is four
        orders of derivatives higher than the $a_2$ Einstein
        sector.
        This is not a deficiency but the correct spectral
        signature: the gauge sector is logarithmically divergent
        (renormalizable-type) while the gravitational sector is
        quadratically divergent.
        The Seeley--DeWitt expansion encodes this difference in
        divergence degree automatically; it does not, by itself,
        predict the finite renormalized couplings.
      \end{remark}

  \section{Induced coupling and the divergence structure}
    \label{sec:hierarchy}

    \subsection{The calculable logarithmic running}
      \label{subsec:induced-coupling}

      Comparing the local logarithmic term~\eqref{eq:entropy-YM} with
      the standard Yang--Mills action
      $S_{\mathrm{YM}} = -(4g_{\mathrm{YM}}^2)^{-1}
      \int F^a_{\mu\nu}F^{a\,\mu\nu}
      \sqrt{g}\,\mathrm{d}^4 x$ (Euclidean signature) identifies the
      \emph{scale dependence} of the inverse coupling, not its value:
      \begin{equation}\label{eq:induced-coupling}
        g_{\mathrm{YM}}^{-2}(\mu)
        \;=\;
        g_{\mathrm{YM}}^{-2}(\mu_0)
        \;+\;
        \frac{I_\rho}{3 \cdot 16\pi^2}\,
        \log\!\left(\frac{\mu_0}{\mu}\right)\,.
      \end{equation}
      The factor of four relative to the coefficient
      in~\eqref{eq:entropy-YM} is the matching itself: that equation gives the coefficient of
      $\int F^a_{\mu\nu}F^{a\,\mu\nu}$, which is identified with $(4g_{\mathrm{YM}}^2)^{-1}$,
      not with $g_{\mathrm{YM}}^{-2}$.
      The constant $g_{\mathrm{YM}}^{-2}(\mu_0)$ is a renormalized
      matching datum, not an output of the construction; only the
      logarithmic coefficient $I_\rho/(3\cdot16\pi^2)$ is computed
      here, and it depends on the representation content through the
      Dynkin index $I_\rho$.
      The four-dimensional logarithmic form is the induced-gauge-theory
      signature of a marginal coupling~\cite{Sakharov1968}; it does not
      by itself fix the sign of the $\beta$-function, and hence does
      not establish asymptotic freedom.

    \subsection{Divergence degree of the two sectors}
      \label{subsec:newton}

      In the companion Gravity paper~\cite{Beau2026h}, the renormalized
      Einstein coefficient $c_{\mathrm{EH}}^{\mathrm{ren}}$ ---
      equivalently Newton's constant $G_N$ --- is likewise a matching
      datum: its value, and even its sign, are fixed by renormalization
      conditions rather than predicted, and any closed-form estimate
      $G_N^{-1}\sim\ell_{\mathrm{sp}}^{-2}$ is not asserted here.
      What the spectral expansion \emph{does} fix is the \emph{degree}
      of the ultraviolet divergence carried by each sector: quadratic
      (order $a_2$) for the Einstein term, logarithmic (order $a_4$)
      for the Yang--Mills term.
      Both sectors are cut off at the same spectral capacity length
      $\ell_{\mathrm{sp}}$~\cite{Beau2026h,Beau2026c}, but this shared
      cutoff sets only the divergence structure, not the finite
      renormalized couplings.

    \subsection{On the gauge--gravity hierarchy}
      \label{subsec:hierarchy}

      Because $g_{\mathrm{YM}}$ and $G_N$ are both renormalized inputs,
      their ratio $G_N g_{\mathrm{YM}}^2$ is \emph{not} predicted here.
      The robust statement is weaker but exact: the
      two couplings sit at different orders of the heat-kernel
      expansion and therefore carry different ultraviolet divergence
      degrees. Turning this qualitative separation into a quantitative
      statement about the ratio would require the finite matching data of both sectors, which the
      spectral expansion does not supply; Q13 likewise treats them as matching data.

    \subsection{Analytical status of [H-color]}
      \label{subsec:hcolor}

      The present paper takes $G_\Pi$ as an input from the
      spectral admissibility sub-programme~\cite{Beau2026q6a,Beau2026a35}.
      The SU(3) sector of $G_\Pi$ is established unconditionally for the
      colour-adapted Cayley graph $\mathrm{Cay}(G_q, S^{(3)}_q)$
      in~\cite{Beau2026a35}.
      For the standard Cayley graph, $[H\text{-color}]_{\mathrm{eff}}$
      (equality of capacity exponents in the large-$q$ limit) is established
      analytically in~\cite{Beau2026a35,Beau2026a36} via the intra-coset
      Vandermonde symmetry (O31) and the modulation-frequency argument (O32).
      Furthermore, $[H\text{-color}]_{\mathrm{pointwise}}$ (exact equality at
      finite~$q$) is proved in O31 (Proposition~4.23,~\cite{Beau2026a35}): each
      O12 fingerprint vector is a single additive character of frequency
      $B = c_1 b_1 + c_2 b_2 + c_3 b_3$, and the orbit dilation
      $B \mapsto \omega B$ is a bijection of $\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z}$, so
      $\sigma_c(n) = \sigma_{\omega c}(n) = \sigma_{\omega^2 c}(n)$ holds exactly.
      The Theorem~\ref{thm:main} of the present paper holds for any
      compact gauge group $G_\Pi$ and is independent of these results;
      they bear only on the identification of the specific group
      $G_\Pi = \mathrm{SU}(3) \times \mathrm{SU}(2) \times \mathrm{U}(1)$.

      The complete programme-level status is:
      \begin{itemize}
        \item $G_\Pi = \mathrm{SU}(2) \times \mathrm{U}(1)$:
        unconditional~\cite{Beau2026q6a};
        \item $G_\Pi = \mathrm{SU}(3)$: unconditional for the
        colour-adapted graph; unconditional at the pointwise level
        ($[H\text{-color}]_{\mathrm{pointwise}}$ proved) for the standard
        graph (Proposition~4.23 of O31~\cite{Beau2026a35});
        \item The finite-$q$ residual variance $R_{\mathrm{var}}$
        is characterised as a modulation bias of order $O(q^{-1})$,
        not an open hypothesis~\cite{Beau2026a36}.
      \end{itemize}

    \subsection{The SU(3) Yang--Mills uniqueness problem}
      \label{subsec:su3-uniqueness}

      The open problem identified in~\cite{Beau2026a35}~(§9.2)
      is the analogue of the Q6a Yang--Mills derivation for SU(3):
      showing that the SU(3) gauge dynamics (structure constants,
      covariant derivative, Yang--Mills equations) emerge uniquely
      from the colour triplet co-admissibility structure.
      This is distinct from the result of the present paper,
      which derives the Yang--Mills equations for any compact $G_\Pi$
      from the $a_4$ heat-kernel coefficient.
      The SU(3) Yang--Mills uniqueness problem concerns the
      \emph{internal} structure of the SU(3) sector, not the
      spectral derivation of the Yang--Mills action.

    \subsection{Relation to Q13}
      \label{subsec:q13}

      The present paper varies the connection at fixed base metric, which isolates the
      Yang--Mills sector (Lemma~\ref{lem:decoupling}). The two sectors are not thereby
      independent: the metric variation of the same $a_4$ gauge term is the Yang--Mills
      energy--momentum tensor (Remark~\ref{rem:no-full-decoupling}), an $a_4$-order back-reaction.
      That back-reaction, and the joint variation, belong to the companion paper Q13
      (Gauge--Gravity Spectral Synthesis), which obtains a \emph{conditional}
      Einstein--Yang--Mills system: its normalisation is fixed by canonical matching, with
      $G_N$, $g_{\mathrm{YM}}$ and the cosmological coefficient entering as independent
      renormalized data rather than predictions, so no numerical value for
      $G_N g_{\mathrm{YM}}^2$ follows.
      Q13 treats the $a_6$ sector only as a representative spanning list of dimension-six
      invariants, modulo integrations by parts and Bianchi identities, with no unique cross-term
      and no non-linear completion asserted.

    \subsection{Spectral stratification as an organisational
    principle}
      \label{subsec:spectral-stratification}

      \begin{remark}
        The spectral stratification identified in this paper ---
        gravity at $a_2$, gauge at $a_4$ --- provides an
        organisational principle that differs fundamentally from
        purely group-theoretic unification schemes.
        In the latter, gravity and gauge interactions are sought
        at the same algebraic level, typically as different
        representations of a common group $G_{\mathrm{unif}}$.
        The present framework suggests instead that they are
        separated by a geometric direction (horizontal versus
        vertical variation on the admissible bundle) and by
        spectral order (two versus four derivatives in the
        heat-kernel expansion).
        Their distinct UV behaviours --- quadratic divergence
        for gravity, logarithmic divergence for gauge --- are
        then consequences of this geometric separation rather
        than independent empirical inputs.
        In this reading, the difficulty of incorporating gravity
        into the gauge-theoretic framework of the Standard Model
        may reflect not a missing symmetry but a difference of
        spectral order: gravity and gauge dynamics are
        not at the same level of the Seeley--DeWitt hierarchy
        of the admissible operator.
        This observation does not imply that strictly algebraic
        unification schemes are incorrect; it suggests that the
        natural organisational space for unification in the
        present framework is spectral and variational rather
        than primarily group-theoretic.
      \end{remark}

  \section{Conclusion}
    \label{sec:conclusion}

    The main result of this paper is the derivation of the
    Yang--Mills equations $D_\mu F^{a\mu\nu} = 0$ as the vertical
    $a_4$ response of the projective spectral entropy functional
    $S_\Pi[g, \mathcal{A}]$, in exact parallel with the derivation
    of the Einstein equations as the horizontal $a_2$ response
    established in~\cite{Beau2026h}.

    The conceptually central observation is the following.
    The Seeley--DeWitt degree of a coefficient encodes the
    ultraviolet divergence structure of the coupling it induces:
    \begin{align*}
      a_2 &\;\longrightarrow\; \text{gravity,}
      &&\text{quadratic UV divergence,}
      &&\text{perturbatively non-renormalizable sector,} \\
      a_4 &\;\longrightarrow\; \text{gauge,}
      &&\text{logarithmic UV divergence,}
      &&\text{renormalizable-type sector.}
    \end{align*}
    What is therefore structural, within the proper-time scheme used here, is the
    \emph{difference in divergence degree} between the two sectors; the contrast is not
    scheme-independent, the zeta-regularized determinant carrying no power divergences at all.
    The numerical gauge--gravity hierarchy $G_N g_{\mathrm{YM}}^2$ is
    \emph{not} an output of this construction: $g_{\mathrm{YM}}$ and
    $G_N$ are renormalized matching data, of which only the
    logarithmic running of $g_{\mathrm{YM}}^{-2}$ is computed here.
    This is in line with the matching-data status of the Einstein sector in the Gravity
    paper~\cite{Beau2026h}.

    Both sectors are cut off at the same spectral capacity length
    $\ell_{\mathrm{sp}}$ and follow from the same non-injective
    projection principle.
    The gauge group $G_\Pi$ is inherited from the admissibility
    sub-programme~\cite{Beau2026q6a,Beau2026a35}, with
    $[H\text{-color}]_{\mathrm{pointwise}}$ established
    in~\cite{Beau2026a35}.

    \emph{Interpretation.} The spectral stratification identified
    here --- gravity at $a_2$, gauge at $a_4$, developed in
    Section~\ref{subsec:spectral-stratification} --- suggests, as an
    interpretive outlook rather than a theorem, that the
    organisational space for unification in this framework may be
    spectral and variational rather than primarily group-theoretic.

    The joint variation --- the conditional Einstein--Yang--Mills system and the
    gauge--gravity back-reaction $T^{\mathrm{YM}}_{\mu\nu}$ --- is addressed in Q13, where the
    couplings likewise remain renormalized matching data.
